About Pauls Valley

Pauls Valley, Oklahoma, is a charming city located in Garvin County with a rich history dating back to the 1800s. Named after Smith Paul, a Chickasaw farmer who established a trading post in the area, the city grew rapidly after the arrival of the Santa Fe Railroad in 1887. Originally a cattle ranching hub, Pauls Valley became known as the "Gateway to Lake Country" due to its proximity to several recreational lakes. The city is famous for being the home of Toy Box, one of the world's largest toy museums, and for its strong agricultural heritage, particularly in wheat and cotton production. Today, Pauls Valley blends its historical charm with modern amenities, serving as a vital community center in south-central Oklahoma with a warm, welcoming spirit that reflects its deep roots and strong sense of community.

The Need for 24/7 Worship

In our modern world, spiritual needs don't follow a schedule. People experience crises, seek comfort, and desire connection with God at all hours of the day and night. Unfortunately, Pauls Valley currently has no church that remains open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This creates a gap in spiritual care for those who work night shifts, experience late-night emergencies, or simply need a quiet place to pray during unconventional hours. A 24/7 open church would serve as a beacon of hope, a sanctuary always available for prayer, meditation, and spiritual guidance. It would demonstrate that God's house is truly always open, reflecting the constant availability of divine love and support.
